Club career

  • Started his youth career at Ridgeway Rovers and endeed up with Tottenham Hotspur in 2004
  • In July 2009 he signed a scholarship contract with Tottenham
  • Kane then had a loan spell with Leyton Orient in the 2010/11 season ending this season with 5 goals in 18 games
  • Kane went on to make his first team appearance for Tottenham in a Europa league game against Scottish team Hearts in 2011
  • Kane then went out on loan to then championship side Milwall in 2011 scoring 9 goals in 27 matches
  • Harry made his premier league debut on the 18th August 2012 against Newcastle
  • On the 31st August 2012 Kane joined Norwich City on loan, returning to Tottenham mid way through the season to then go on to join leicester City on loan for the remainder of the season
  • Harry Kane scored his first Tottenham goal in the 2013/14 season against Hull City in the league cup
  • On the 7th April 2014 Kane was handed his premier league debut by Tim Sherwood where he scored his first premier league goal in a 5-1 victory over Sunderland
  • In the 2014/15 season Harry Kane performed exceptionally earning himself the PFA Young Player of The Year Award
  • Kane got his 100th goal for Tottenham on the 9th September 2017 on his 169th appearance
  • On the 11th March 2023 Harry Kane became Tottenhams all time top goal scorer over taking Jimmy Greaves with his 269th goal for Tottenham
  • Harry Kane left Tottenham in the summer of 2023 to join FC Bayern Munich in Germany for a fee of 100m euros, finishing his Tottenham career with 280 goals
  • Harry Kane made his Bayern debut in a 3-0 defeat to RB Leipzig in the 2023 DFL-Supercup
  • Kane Scored his first Bayern Munich goal on the opening day of 2023/24 season in a 4-0 win against Werder Bremen

International Career

  • Kane scored his first England goal against Lithuania on his debut
  • On the 30th March 2015 Harry Kane made his first England start against Italy
  • Kane captained England on the 10th June 2017 against Scotland in a world cup qualifier
  • On the 23rd March Kane scored against Italy making it his 53rd goal for Enlgand overtaking Wayne Rooney as Englands all time record goalscorer

Random Fact!

He was awarded the MBE (Member of the Order of the British Empire) in the 2019 Queen's New Years Honours List for his services to Football. He is the Captain of the England Football Team in London, England.
